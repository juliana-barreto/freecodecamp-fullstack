Brazilian Carrot Cake

Carrot cake is a simple and practical option for breakfast, an afternoon snack, or a special occasion with family and friends. This recipe is made in a blender and ready in under 1 hour, making it ideal for when you're short on time in the kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 3 medium carrots, peeled and chopped (about 2 cups / 300g)
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup vegetable oil (240ml)
  • 2 cups granulated sugar (400g)
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (240g)
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder (15g)

For the chocolate frosting:

  • 1 can sweetened condensed milk (395g)
  • 4 tablespoons cocoa powder (unsweetened)
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter (15g)

Optional: Sprinkles or grated chocolate for decoration

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C). Grease and flour a 9x13-inch (23x33cm) baking pan.
  2. In a blender, combine the chopped carrots, eggs, oil, and sugar. Blend until smooth (about 2-3 minutes).
  3. Pour the mixture into a large bowl. Sift in the flour and baking powder, then gently fold until just combined (do not overmix).
  4. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and bake for 30–40 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  5. Let the cake cool completely before frosting.
  6. In a small saucepan, combine the condensed milk, cocoa powder, and butter. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring constantly, until thickened (about 5–7 minutes).
  7. Spread the warm frosting over the cooled cake. Add sprinkles or grated chocolate if desired.
